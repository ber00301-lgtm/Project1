Mushroom

Mushroom-based materials are a highly sustainable alternative to conventional plastics and foams. They are made from mycelium—the root structure of fungi—which grows quickly on agricultural waste such as corn husks, straw, or sawdust, turning leftover biomass into a useful product. Unlike plastics, mushroom materials require minimal energy and water to produce, and they naturally biodegrade in soil within weeks or months without leaving toxic residues.
