Seaweed

PHA (Polyhydroxyalkanoates) is a type of bioplastic made by microorganisms through the fermentation of plant sugars or oils. Unlike traditional plastics made from fossil fuels, PHA is biodegradable and compostable, breaking down naturally in soil and marine environments. It’s used as a sustainable alternative for packaging, single-use items, and medical materials because it offers the durability of plastic without long-term pollution.
